# THE ARCHIVES OF ORBIS: WORLD BIBLE
*(v3.2 - High Fantasy Edition)*

---

## I. COSMOLOGY: THE SIX SPHERES
The universe is structured as a descending cascade of realities. Existence flows downwards from the Origin.

### Sphere 0: The Origin (The Primeval Truth)
- **Nature**: Pure Concept and Absolute Truth. The "First Words" spoken by the Architect.
- **Inhabitants**: None.
- **Function**: This is where the "Archetypes" (The True Names of Fire, Time, Gravity) exist in their rawest form.

### Sphere 1: The Celestial Lattice (The Divine Throne)
- **Nature**: A prism dimension.
- **Inhabitants**: The High Deities (Rank 1).
- **Function**: The Gods sit upon Thrones that act as lenses, refracting the blinding light of the Origin into manageable forces (e.g., Raw Power becomes "Sunlight"). Gods cannot physically enter lower spheres without shattering them; they must use Avatars.

### Sphere 2: Orbis (The Mortal Coil)
- **Nature**: The Material World.
- **Inhabitants**: Mortals, Monsters, Ascenders.
- **Function**: The "Harmonic Zone" where Mana (Possibility) and Matter (Reality) coexist, allowing for life and magic.

### Sphere 3: The Spirit Echo (The Dream)
- **Nature**: A subconscious reflection of Orbis.
- **Inhabitants**: Ghosts, Spirits, Dream-walkers.
- **Function**: Used for telepathy, astral projection, and illusion magic.

### Sphere 4: The Iron Realm (The Stilled World / Earth)
- **Nature**: A dimension of pure Matter and Unyielding Law, severed from the Origin.
- **Inhabitants**: The Magicless.
- **Function**: Souls from this sphere are "forged" and rigid, unlike the fluid souls of Orbis.

### Sphere 5: The Abyss (The Cesspit)
- **Nature**: A cosmic sinkhole and "Spiritual Dross" heap.
- **Inhabitants**: Demons, Fallen Gods, The Twisted.
- **Function**: It seeks to rise and consume Sphere 2. Crucially, the Abyss is a direct byproduct of Sphere 1. When Gods filter the "Primeval Truth" to make it safe for mortals, the chaotic, rejected conceptual debris (slag) is cast down here. The brighter the Gods shine, the more "rot" is pumped into the Abyss. The Gods are creating the very evil they fight.

## II. THE MAGIC SYSTEM: SIGIL ASCENSION
Magic is not a class system; it is a "Soul Inscription" system where individuals carve Symbols of Power (Boons) into their spiritual essence.

### 1. The Structure of Power (The Countdown)
Advancement is measured by the complexity and Density of the Soul. The Ascenders Guild classifies these levels into distinct tiers.

- **Rank 10 - Rank 9 (The Filter / Copper)**: The stage of "Aspirants." They have unlocked their Soul Lattice but posses low density. Most struggle to stabilize their Anchor, and attempting a Reach or Greed slot often kills them.
- **Rank 8 - Rank 7 (The Survivors / Bronze)**: Competent users who have stabilized their Anchor and often utilize a Reach. They are the standard foot-soldiers of the magic world.
- **Rank 6 - Rank 5 (The Professionals / Iron)**: Users who have mastered the synergy between their slots. They possess a "Full Deck" (Anchor, Reach, and Greed) working in harmony.
- **Rank 4 (The Elite / Silver)**: National Assets. They have refined their Boons to remove inefficiencies.
- **Rank 3 (The Heavyweights / Platinum)**: The Threshold of Soul Gravitas. As a soul fills with powerful Boons, it gains metaphysical mass. High-ranking individuals warp reality just by existing (e.g., glass cracks, air thins, weaker beings feel "Primal Dread"). Because of this, they are often forced into isolation.
- **Rank 2 (The Sovereigns / Gold)**: Walking Natural Disasters.
- **Rank 1 (True God / Black)**: Complete fusion with an Archetype in Sphere 1.

### 2. The Rule of Three (Soul Slots)
At every Rank, an Ascender has the potential to fill 3 Slots. However, they must be carved **Sequentially**, and the risk increases exponentially with each step.

1. **The Anchor (The Foundation)**: The first slot. Essential for stabilizing the soul at the current Rank. **Risk: Moderate.**
2. **The Reach (The Extension)**: The second slot. Cannot be carved until the Anchor is stable. Adds significant strain to the soul. **Risk: High.**
3. **The Greed (The Catalyst)**: The third slot. A volatile power source. **Risk: Extreme.** Most Ascenders avoid this slot as it frequently leads to corruption or death.

### 3. Methods of Acquisition
To gain a Boon, one must carve it using one of three methods:

- **Method 1: The Path of Will (Epiphany)**: Using raw mana and deep Understanding of a concept to force it into existence. Extremely dangerous; requires perfect comprehension of the concept's True Name.
- **Method 2: The Path of Rites (Alchemy)**: Using ancient recipes and physical catalysts (Monster Cores) to stabilize the mana. This is the standard method.
- **Method 3: The Path of Grace (Divine Grant)**: A God stamps the Boon directly onto the soul. Safe, but creates servitude.

### 4. The Economy of Magic
- **Monster Cores**: The crystallized hearts of monsters. They contain "Wild Mana" and genetic memory. Used as batteries or ingredients for Rituals.
- **The Twisted**: Ascenders who failed a ritual or filled a "Greed" slot incorrectly. They suffer physical mutations or insanity and are social outcasts.

## III. HISTORY: THE THREE EPOCHS

### Epoch 1: The Age of Predation (The Era of Titans)
- **The World**: Ruled by Progenitors—massive, biological manifestations of magic (True Dragons, Behemoths, World Trees).
- **Evolution**: Magic functioned biologically. The Progenitors evolved into the Old Gods.
- **The End**: The "Ascension War." The strongest monsters fought for the limited Thrones in Sphere 1.

### Epoch 2: The War of Shackles (The Human Uprising)
- **The Turning Point**: Humanity, previously weak scavengers, discovered Alchemy. They learned to hunt monsters, eat their cores, and steal their powers.
- **The Conflict**: Human Heroes (The Forge Father, The Sun King, etc.) killed or subjugated the Old Gods using their adaptability to counter the Progenitors' raw strength.
- **The Legacy**: Civilization was built on the ruins of the Progenitors.

### Epoch 3: The Age of Silence (Current Era)
- **Status**: A stagnated "High Magic" era.
- **The Secret Truce**: The Human Gods cannot rule alone. They maintain a secret peace treaty with the surviving Ancient Monsters (The Hidden Progenitors) who dwell in the shadows of the world.

## IV. GEOGRAPHY: THE PLANET ORBIS
**Scale**: A planet roughly 1.5x the size of Earth.  
**Structure**: Divided into the Central Continent (The Pantheon), the Outer Lands, and the Western Supercontinent, separated by vast oceans and magical barriers.

### A. THE CENTRAL PANTHEON (The Civilized Kingdoms)
The geopolitical heart of Orbis, where the major deities hold sway.

#### Aurelia (The Golden West)
- **Patron**: Solaris (The Sun King).
- **Domain**: Agriculture, Harvest, Light.
- **Geography**: The "Breadbasket of the World." Endless golden wheat fields and eternal sunlight in the core provinces.
- **Key Feature**: The Sun Spire (Capital Sanctum). It controls the weather and creates an "Eternal Noon."

#### Ferrum (The Iron South)
- **Patron**: Vulcanis (The Forge Father).
- **Domain**: Industry, Craft, Fire.
- **Geography**: A jagged peninsula of smog, active volcanoes, and factories.
- **Key Feature**: The Great Furnace (Artificial Volcano).

#### Valoria (The Heartland)
- **Patron**: Bellum (The Strategist).
- **Domain**: Combat, Strategy, Adventure.
- **Geography**: A highly militarized nation sitting centrally between Arcanum and Ferrum.

#### Arcanum (The Sanctum)
- **Patron**: Magus (The High Scholar).
- **Domain**: Knowledge, Wisdom, Arcane Law.
- **Geography**: A central, protected valley west of Valoria. It houses the world's greatest libraries.

#### Bastion (The Northern Shield)
- **Patron**: Aegis (The Guardian).
- **Domain**: Defense, Fortification.
- **Geography**: A rugged, fortified coastline in the North-Central region.

#### Nyxia (The Shadowed North)
- **Patron**: Nox (The Night Mother).
- **Domain**: Shadows, Spirits, Secrets.
- **Geography**: The North-West corner. A land of perpetual twilight.

#### Lunaris (The Silver Coast)
- **Patron**: Selene (The Moon Mistress).
- **Domain**: Tides, Illusions, Navigation.
- **Geography**: The Eastern coast. A land of silver cities and bioluminescent waters.

#### Terragard (The High Reaches)
- **Patron**: Terrus (The Earth Shaper).
- **Domain**: Architecture, Masonry, Stability.
- **Geography**: The North-East mountains.

#### Aeterna (The Fated Point)
- **Patron**: Fatum (The Weaver).
- **Domain**: Prophecy, History, Time.
- **Geography**: An isolated peninsula in the Far East.

### B. THE OUTER LANDS
Regions existing on the periphery of Pantheon influence.

- **Xylos (The Wild South)**
    - **Status**: No Patron God.
    - **Geography**: Dense jungles transitioning into vast savannahs south of Ferrum. Ancient craters and "Scars" (Dead Mana Zones).
    - **Culture**: A wild, nature-aligned land that rejects the Pantheon.

- **Veridia (The Life Isle)**
    - **Patron**: Gaia (The Vitality Mother).
    - **Geography**: An isolated island east of Ferrum.
    - **Description**: A lush, overgrown jungle where flora is gigantic and aggressive.

- **Aethelgard (The Storm Archipelago)**
    - **Patron**: Tempest (The Storm Queen).
    - **Geography**: A fragmented archipelago in the Far East Ocean.
    - **Description**: The Trade Federation. The world's greatest naval power.

- **Requiem (The Southern Continent)**
    - **Patron**: Mortis (The Silent Keeper).
    - **Geography**: The South Pole region. A frozen, glacial continent shrouded in eternal magical darkness.
    - **Status**: Isolated and Forbidden. Home to Vampires and Necromancers.

- **Terra Incognita (The Western Continent)**
    - **Status**: Sealed and unexplored since Epoch 1.
    - **Geography**: A massive supercontinent west of the barrier.

## V. ECONOMY, CURRENCY & FINANCE (The Aether-Standard)
In Orbis, wealth is not just a representation of value; it is a storage of potential energy. The economy runs on the "Conductivity Standard."

### 1. THE CURRENCY: THE LIVING COIN
Coins in Orbis are minted from specific conductive alloys capable of holding a Mana Charge. A coin has two values: its Face Value (Commerce) and its Energy Value (Utility).

- **The Mechanic (The Flicker)**: A standard Gold Crown can function as a low-grade battery. In an emergency, an Ascender or Magitech user can "drain" the coin to power a device (like a Spark-Baton) or jump-start a spell.
- **Charged State ("Glow")**: Fresh from the bank. The coin feels warm and hums faintly. Worth full value.
- **Drained State ("Dull")**: Used as fuel. The coin turns cold, grey, and heavy. It is still legal tender, but merchants value it at 80% because they must pay a fee to recharge it.

#### The Denominations
- **Tier 1: Copper "Bit" (1 Value)**: Too impure to hold a charge. Used strictly for buying bread/ale.
- **Tier 2: Silver "Stag" (100 Bits)**: Holds a weak charge. Can power a reading lamp for an hour.
- **Tier 3: Gold "Crown" (100 Stags)**: The Standard. Holds enough charge to fire a "Sun-Spark" emitter twice.
- **Tier 4: Platinum "Royal" (100 Crowns)**: High-Density Capacity. Used to power vehicles or heavy machinery in a pinch.

### 2. THE BANKING SYSTEM: THE THREE TIERS
Banking in Orbis is divided by social caste. The movement of money is controlled by the Dvar (Dwarves) and the Guild.

#### A. THE GUILD LEDGER (The Universal Bank)
- **Status**: Exclusive. Available only to registered Ascenders (Rank 10+).
- **The Tech**: Utilizes Quantum-Entangled Vaults. A coin deposited in the Guild Hall of Valoria can be instantly withdrawn in the Guild Outpost of Bastion.
- **The Benefit**: Security. Ascenders travel into dangerous lands; carrying physical gold is a liability. The Ledger allows them to travel "light" and withdraw funds at any Guild branch.
- **The Limitation**: The Guild does not give loans. They are a vault, not a lender.

#### B. THE IRON BANKS (The Regional Powers)
Civilian banking is dominated by massive, region-specific institutions. These are the engines of the industrial world, controlling mortgages, factory loans, and war bonds.

- **The Deep-Silver Syndicate (Global / Dvar-Run)**:
    - **HQ**: The Financial District of every Capital.
    - **Specialty**: Corporate Finance & The Rail Network.
    - **Power**: They own the Aether-Rail. If a nation defaults on its debt, the Syndicate shuts down the trains, freezing the country's logistics instantly.
- **The Golden Sheaf (Aurelia)**:
    - **Specialty**: Futures Trading. They bet on the weather. Farmers take loans against their future harvest. If the Sun King shines too bright and burns the crop, the bank owns the farm.
- **The Iron Reserve (Ferrum)**:
    - **Specialty**: Industry & Hazard Pay. They offer high-interest loans to factory owners to buy raw mana. They also manage "Widow Funds" for the families of Ash-Forged who melt on the job.

#### C. LOCAL LENDERS (The Loan Sharks)
In the Undercities and rural villages, the "Banks" are wealthy individuals or crime syndicates.
- **The Power of Borrowing**: Local banks hold the real power over the common man. Because High Draconic Contracts (see Linguistics) are binding by physics, defaulting on a loan is not a civil matter—it is a magical consequence.
- **The "Collateral" Curse**: Small banks use "Blood-Lending." If you borrow money to buy a fishing boat, you sign with a drop of blood. If you miss payments, the contract might physically paralyze your arm or blind you until the debt is paid.

### 3. BANKING OPERATIONS: THE RECHARGE CYCLE
Banks are not just vaults; they are Refineries.
- **The Intake**: Merchants bring bags of "Dull" (drained) coins to the bank at the end of the day.
- **The Process**: The bank employs mages or uses massive industrial Mana-Presses (powered by raw Monster Cores) to inject energy back into the coins.
- **The Profit**: The "Minting Fee." The bank takes a 5-10% cut for the service of recharging the currency.
- **Example**: You bring 100 Dull Crowns. The Bank gives you 90 Glowing Crowns back.
- **The Economy of War**: During monster sieges, banks shut their doors to hoard energy. A "Bank Run" in Orbis implies people rushing to withdraw charged coins to use as ammunition.

## VI. RACES
**RACIAL COMPENDIUM (v4.0)**
- **Status**: Finalized Demographic Census
- **Era**: The Age of Silence (Year 3E 1625)
- **Total Global Population**: Approx. 950,000,000

This document outlines the sentient species of the Surface World, ranked by population density. It reflects the current Industrial Magitech era, where biology dictates one’s role in the Great Machine of society.

### 1. THE HUMANS (The Dominant Line)
- **Population**: ~522,500,000 (55%)
- **Primary Regions**: Aurelia, Valoria, Bastion, Arcanum.
- **The Concept**: The "Jack-of-All-Trades." Humans lack inherent biological magic or claws, which makes their souls "empty vessels." This allows them to absorb any type of Elemental Boon (Fire, Time, Gravity), making them the most versatile Mages.
- **Role**: The Drivers. They are the Kings, the Generals, the Shopkeepers, and the Farmers. They possess the Ambition that fuels the era.
- **Racial Trait**: The Blank Slate. +10% Experience Gain in any chosen Magic School.

### 2. THE MURIDAE (The Pipe-Walkers)
- **Population**: ~142,500,000 (15%)
- **Primary Regions**: The Undercities of all major metropolises (especially Ferrum and Arcanum).
- **The Concept**: Civilized, bipedal Rat-Folk. They are not savages; they are hyper-clean, communal, and obsessed with efficiency. They evolved in the ruins of the Old World, learning to fix what others broke.
- **Aesthetic**: They wear heavy rubber wading boots, canvas overalls with many pockets, and brass "Breather Masks" to filter smog and scents. They carry wrenches like swords.
- **Social Role**: The Urban Backbone. They run the Sanitation, Recycling, and Transit networks. They maintain the sewers and the steam tunnels. "If the Rats strike, the city drowns in filth."
- **Racial Trait**: Chem-Sense. Can smell toxins, poisons, and magic leaks. Immunity to disease.

### 3. THE GEODIN (The Stone-Skins)
- **Population**: ~95,000,000 (10%)
- **Primary Regions**: Terragard (Native), Valoria.
- **The Concept**: A silicon-based lifeform. They look like living statues of marble, granite, or slate, growing crystal "hair" and "beards." They move slowly but with unstoppable momentum.
- **Aesthetic**: They struggle with cloth (it rips), so they wear heavy leather aprons, utility belts, and custom steel-plate suits for formal occasions. They do not wear shoes.
- **Social Role**: The Builders & Breakers. They are the architects who build the skyscrapers and the Heavy Infantry who act as living riot shields for the police.
- **Racial Trait**: Lithic Density. Natural Armor Class is high. They cannot swim (they sink instantly) but can walk on the bottom of rivers.

### 4. THE DVAR (The Deep-Kin)
- **Population**: ~76,000,000 (8%)
- **Primary Regions**: The Financial Districts of Capital Cities.
- **The Concept**: Dwarves who traded their pickaxes for checkbooks. They realized that owning the mine is better than working in it. They are the masters of "Stable Enchantment."
- **Aesthetic**: The Rail Barons. Impeccable three-piece tweed suits, gold pocket watches, monocles, and bowler hats. Their beards are groomed with expensive oils.
- **Social Role**: The Elite. They own the Aether-Rail networks, the Banks, and the Gunsmithing Guilds. They are the middle-class aristocracy.
- **Racial Trait**: Stone-Lung. Immune to inhalation hazards (smog, smoke, gas).

### 5. THE ASH-FORGED (The Cinder-Kin)
- **Population**: ~47,500,000 (5%)
- **Primary Regions**: Ferrum (The Iron South).
- **The Concept**: Artificial lifeforms sculpted from clay and fueled by an internal "Heart-Fire." They look like cracked grey statues with magma glowing beneath the skin. They emit constant heat.
- **Aesthetic**: They often wear heavy iron containment suits to prevent setting furniture on fire. In the factories, they work naked, their skin glowing white-hot.
- **Social Role**: The Living Engines. They work the hazardous jobs: inside blast furnaces, handling radioactive mana-waste, and powering boilers. They are often treated as "Equipment" rather than citizens.
- **Racial Trait**: Thermal Core. Immune to Fire damage. Heals when exposed to extreme heat. Water acts as acid to them.

### 6. THE URSINE (The Great-Coats)
- **Population**: ~28,500,000 (3%)
- **Primary Regions**: Bastion (North), Aethelgard (Docks).
- **The Concept**: Massive, bipedal Bear-Folk. They are 7-8 feet tall, covered in thick fur. They are stoic, family-oriented, and terrifyingly strong.
- **Aesthetic**: They wear "Great Coats" (oversized trench coats), flat caps, and wide trousers held up by suspenders. They usually smoke pipes.
- **Social Role**: The Muscle. Stevedores (loading ships), Bodyguards, Bouncers, and Shock Troops. They are the "Gentle Giants" of the pub scene until a fight starts.
- **Racial Trait**: Hibernation Metabolism. Can survive for weeks without food. Resistance to Cold and Bludgeoning damage.

### 7. THE NEREID (The Tide-Born)
- **Population**: ~19,000,000 (2%)
- **Primary Regions**: Lunaris, Aethelgard.
- **The Concept**: Amphibious humanoids with translucent, bioluminescent skin and large dark eyes. They need moisture to survive.
- **Aesthetic**: They wear "Wet-Suits" made of oiled rubber and carry hydration mist-sprayers. Their fashion involves pearls and coral.
- **Social Role**: The Mariners. They dominate the shipping trade, pearl diving, and naval warfare. They are often isolationists, sticking to the coasts.
- **Racial Trait**: Aquatic. Breathe water and air. Swim speed equals run speed.

### 8. THE DUSKIN (The Faded)
- **Population**: ~9,500,000 (1%)
- **Primary Regions**: Scattered in Urban Rookeries.
- **The Concept**: High Elves who have lost their connection to nature and magic. They are tall, gaunt, and sickly, often addicted to "Mana-Vapor."
- **Aesthetic**: Gothic Tragedy. Long black coats, high collars to hide their thin necks, and tinted spectacles (their eyes are sensitive). They look like faded aristocracy.
- **Social Role**: The Precision Craftsmen. Despite their frailty, they have the steadiest hands. They are the Watchmakers, Jewelers, and Lens-Grinders.
- **Racial Trait**: Mana-Sight. Can naturally see magical auras without casting a spell.

### 9. THE OTHERS (The Fringes)
- **Population**: ~9,500,000 (1%)
- **Social Status**: Varied (From Mythic Nobility to Illegal Outcasts).
These species are divided into four sub-categories based on their origin and standing in society.

#### A. THE HIGH-BLOOD (The Rare Elite)
Beings of immense power or ancient lineage who hold positions of privilege despite their low numbers.

- **1. The Draconians (The Scale-Lords)**
    - **Role**: Elite Nobility / High Officers.
    - **Description**: Tall, humanoid dragons with wings and breath weapons. They view themselves as the rightful heirs of the Progenitors.
    - **Status**: They are the 0.01%. They don't work; they own. Most live in the highest penthouses of the Spire.

- **2. The Vitruvian (The Prism-Skins)**
    - **Origin**: Rare scholars from Arcanum born near high-concentration mana crystals.
    - **Appearance**: Their skin is translucent and geometric, acting like living glass.
    - **Ability**: Refraction. They naturally deflect light and low-tier magical projectiles. They are prized as diplomats because it is hard to assassinate them with lasers or spells.

- **3. The Astrals (Star-Touched)**
    - **Origin**: Albinos born in Aeterna during specific celestial alignments.
    - **Appearance**: Their hair floats in a personal zero-gravity field, and their eyes reflect the night sky.
    - **Ability**: The Sight. They possess natural intuition for Prophecy magic. The Banks use them to predict stock market crashes.

#### B. THE INDUSTRIAL ASSETS (The Specialized Labor)
Species hired for specific biological traits that machines cannot replicate.

- **4. The Myrmidon (Hive-Kin)**
    - **Origin**: Insectoids from the Outer Lands.
    - **Appearance**: Four-armed, chitin-plated warriors.
    - **Role**: Shock Troops. Prized in Valoria because they lack individual fear and possess telepathic coordination. A squad of Myrmidons moves with perfect synchronization, like a single organism.

- **5. The Ophidians (Serpent-Born)**
    - **Origin**: The Southern Jungles (bordering Xylos).
    - **Appearance**: Humanoids with snake tails instead of legs and hooded necks.
    - **Role**: The Chemists. Immune to all toxins and naturally secreting venom, they are the world's leading Alchemists and Apothecaries.

- **6. The Gargoyles (Sun-Eaters)**
    - **Origin**: Originally stone constructs carved to guard cathedrals, they gained biological sentience through centuries of solar absorption.
    - **Role**: Aerial Sentries. They sit motionless on the rooftops of banks and skyscrapers for days, needing only sunlight to survive. When a thief appears, they strike.

- **7. The Harpies (Rust-Feathers)**
    - **Origin**: Scavengers of the mountain peaks.
    - **Appearance**: Bird-women with metallic, razor-sharp feathers that look like rusted iron.
    - **Role**: Cheap Couriers. They fly in weather that grounds airships. They are often employed to deliver illicit packages.

- **8. The Mycoids (Rot-Walkers)**
    - **Origin**: Veridia.
    - **Appearance**: Sentient fungal colonies that mimic humanoid shapes.
    - **Role**: Farmers. They communicate with plant life chemically. They are hired in Aurelia to manage high-yield crops, though they smell of damp earth.

#### C. THE CURSED & MUTATED (The Urban Legends)
Species created by magical accidents or Abyssal corruption. Often feared.

- **9. The Umbral (The Faceless)**
    - **Origin**: Descendants of humans trapped in Sphere 3 (The Spirit Echo) who phased back incorrectly.
    - **Appearance**: Features are blurred like a smeared photograph. They have no face unless they focus their will.
    - **Affinity**: Static Stealth. If they stand still, they naturally erase themselves from the observer's attention.
    - **Status**: Illegal / Kill-on-Sight in most nations due to their spy potential. Legal only in Nyxia (Home) and Valoria (State-Sanctioned Assassins).

- **10. The Wretches (Dust-Eaters)**
    - **Origin**: Mutated humans born in the deepest industrial slums or near "Mana Scars."
    - **Appearance**: Hairless, pale, with glowing veins visible beneath the skin.
    - **Role**: Hazmat Cleanup. They feed on radiation. They are hired to enter unstable reactor cores or eat hazardous magical waste.

- **11. The Ogre-Magi (The Gemini)**
    - **Origin**: A genetic anomaly among the Giant-kin.
    - **Appearance**: Two-headed giants.
    - **Ability**: Dual-Soul. Because they have two heads, they have two distinct souls. This allows them to hold two Anchor Slots (Rank 10) and cast conflicting elements (e.g., Fire and Ice) simultaneously without exploding.

#### D. THE DEEP ONES (The Underground)
Species that rarely come to the surface.

- **12. The Kobolds (The Deep-Scrapers)**
    - **Origin**: The Underworld.
    - **Appearance**: Small, dog-like lizards.
    - **Role**: They exist below the Muridae (Rats). While Rats run the sewers, Kobolds run the Deep Mines near the planet's core. They occasionally surface to trade rare gems for surface technology.

## VIII. THE GREAT AXIOMS (The Laws of Reality)
These are the immutable laws that govern the interaction between the Spheres and the Soul.

## XVII. THE CELESTIAL MECHANICS & CALENDAR
While the passage of time mirrors Earth for the sake of mortal sanity (24 hours, 365 days), the physical mechanics of Orbis are unique to accommodate its massive scale.

### 1. THE SCALE: THE GEODE THEORY
Orbis has a radius 1.5x that of Earth. Under normal physics, this would result in crushing gravity (approx. 1.5 Gs). However, the planet is habitable due to its unique internal structure.
- **The Honeycomb Crust**: Orbis is not a solid sphere of molten iron. The crust and mantle are porous, riddled with continent-sized cavern systems, "Mana Veins" (hollow tunnels where magical gas flows), and vast subterranean oceans.
- **Resulting Gravity (1.1 G)**: Because the planet is less dense than Earth, gravity is only slightly higher.
- **Effect**: Falls are deadlier, arrows drop faster, and siege engines must be calibrated with heavier counterweights. Humans on Orbis are naturally stockier and denser-boned than Earth humans.

### 2. THE CALENDAR (The Imperial Standard)
To maintain order, the Guild and Kings adhere to a synchronized "Atomic Clock" based on the Sun’s mana pulse.
- **The Day**: 24 Hours.
- **The Year**: 12 Months (Named after the 12 Primary Archetypes).
- **The Seasons**: Standard 4-season cycle (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) driven by the planet's Axial Tilt (23.5 degrees).

### 3. THE ROTATION: THE CORIOLIS PROBLEM
A planet 1.5x the size of Earth spinning in 24 hours has an equatorial velocity of ~1,500 mph (compared to Earth's ~1,000 mph).
- **The Threat**: This rapid spin generates massive Coriolis Forces. Without intervention, the "Trade Winds" would be constant Category 5 hurricanes capable of stripping the soil off the continents.
- **The Solution (Divine Dampeners)**: The Gods act as atmospheric brakes. The "Sanctuaries" (e.g., The Sun Spire, The Great Furnace) project vertical pressure fields that disrupt the horizontal wind shear.
- **Narrative Consequence**: If a God's power wavers (or a Sanctuary is besieged), the "True Wind" returns. A "Storm" in Orbis isn't just rain; it's the planet trying to tear the city apart with wind speed.

### 4. THE SUN: SOLIS (The Mana Star)
- **Nature**: A physical tear in the sky leading to the Elemental Plane of Fire. It does not burn fuel; it bleeds energy.
- **The Solar Pulse**:
    - **Sunrise**: Mana density is low ("The Ebb").
    - **Zenith (High Noon)**: Mana density is at maximum ("The Flood"). Spells are volatile; industrial mana-furnaces run at peak efficiency.
    - **Sunset**: Mana recedes.
- **Visuals**: At Noon, the sun doesn't just look yellow; it has a visible, shimmering "Corona" of raw magic that distorts the air around it.

### 5. THE TWIN MOONS
Orbis has two moons that govern tides and biological rhythms.
- **A. LUNARA (The White Eye)**
    - **Nature**: Large, pearl-white, tidally locked.
    - **Orbit**: Regular 28-day cycle.
    - **Function**: Stabilizes the planet's wobble and controls the ocean tides. It is the "Calendar Moon."
- **B. THE STRANGER (The Shard)**
    - **Nature**: A jagged, reddish asteroid captured in a highly elliptical, erratic orbit. It looks like a broken spear tip.
    - **Orbit**: Chaos. It appears for 3-5 days, then vanishes into deep space for weeks or months.
    - **The Agitation**: When The Stranger is visible, it reflects a specific wavelength of "Red Mana" that irritates the Monster Cores.
    - **Effect**: Monsters become aggressive and leave their territories.
    - **Culture**: "Red Moon Nights" are mandatory curfews in rural villages. No trade convoys move when The Stranger is watching.

### 6. METEOROLOGY: REGIONAL CLIMATES
Because the Gods suppress the "True Wind," weather is semi-artificial and highly regional.
- **The Golden Belt (Aurelia)**: Eternal Summer. The Sun King pushes clouds away.
- **The Grey Cap (Ferrum)**: Artificial humidity. The smog trap keeps heat in, preventing the region from freezing despite the ash blocking the sun.
- **The Twilight North (Nyxia)**: The Goddess Nox allows the natural cold to enter but dampens the wind, creating a silent, snowy stillness rather than a blizzard.

## XX. LINGUISTICS (v4.0)

**The Four Tongues of the Third Epoch**
Language in Orbis is not just a means of communication; it is a measure of closeness to the "Truth." All languages stem from the Progenitors, but as they branched out, they lost their magical potency.

### I. THE ROOT: HIGH DRACONIC (The Binding Tongue)
*"Words are Chains."*
- **Status**: Ancient / Restricted.
- **The Nature**: This is the language of Sphere 1 (The Celestial Lattice). It does not describe reality; it commands it. The grammar is based on the flow of Mana.
- **Current Speakers**:
    - **True Dragons**: (The few who remain slumbering).
    - **High Draconians**: The elite nobility of the Draconian race still teach this to their children, viewing it as a divine right.
    - **Rank 3+ Ascenders**: Required learning to cast High-Tier spells.
- **The Contract Mechanic (The Blood-Script)**:
    - High Draconic is used for Soul-Binding Contracts. If two parties sign a contract written in Draconic Runes, the terms are enforced by the Laws of Physics.
    - **Example**: If a merchant signs a Draconic contract to pay a debt and refuses, his pockets might physically catch fire, or his vault might unseal itself.
    - **Cost**: Writing in High Draconic requires special ink (infused with gold or blood) because the paper will burn otherwise.

### II. THE MAIN BRANCH: IMPERIAL (The World Tongue)
*"Order through Syntax."*
- **Status**: Global Standard (60% of Population).
- **Primary Region**: The Central Pantheon (Aurelia, Valoria, Arcanum).
- **Speakers**: Humans, Dvar (Dwarves), Draconians (Public usage).
- **The Concept**: The civilized descendant of Draconic. It stripped away the magical vibrations to create a language safe for commerce and law. It is rigid, verbose, and polite.
- **The Vibe**: Victorian English / Latin. It creates a sense of bureaucracy and history.
- **Use Case**:
    - Newspapers (The Aurelian Times).
    - Legal Codes.
    - International Diplomacy.
    - **Sample Phrase**: "The Magistratum hereby decrees that all citizens must observe the curfew."

### III. THE SOUTH BRANCH: FERRO (The Spark Tongue)
*"Efficiency is Survival."*
- **Status**: Industrial / Working Class (25% of Population).
- **Primary Region**: Ferrum (The Iron South), The Undercity, The Mines.
- **Speakers**: The Muridae (Rat-Folk), Ash-Forged, Factory Workers.
- **The Concept**: Evolved in the loud, dangerous environment of the Industrial Revolution. It took Draconic and chopped it into short, percussive sounds. It eliminates "filler words" (the, a, is, are) to convey information instantly.
- **The Vibe**: Germanic / Morse Code / Street Slang. Harsh consonants, compound words, and rapid delivery.
- **Use Case**:
    - Blueprints & Engineering Schematics.
    - Telegraphy (It transmits faster than Imperial).
    - Criminal Codes (Thieves' Cant).
    - **Sample Phrase**: "Pipe-pressure critical. Valve-lock jammed. Run."

### IV. THE NORTH BRANCH: SYLVIC (The Deep Tongue)
*"The Old Ways Remain."*
- **Status**: Traditional / Rural (15% of Population).
- **Primary Region**: Bastion, Lunaris, Terragard, and The Outer Lands.
- **Speakers**: The Geodin (Stone-Skins), Ursine (Bear-Folk), Nereid (Sea-Folk), Elves.
- **The Concept**: The branch that stayed close to nature. It retained the musicality of High Draconic but lost the command. It is a tonal language where meaning changes based on the pitch (singing vs. speaking).
- **The Vibe**: Gaelic / Nordic. Melodic, slow, and heavy with metaphor. It has many words for "Stone," "Ice," and "Tide," but no word for "money" or "factory."
- **Use Case**:
    - Oral History & Songs.
    - Talking to Spirits/Ancestors (Sphere 3).
    - Naval commands.
    - **Sample Phrase**: "The stone sleeps, but the winter wakes."

## XXI. VALORIA LORE
**Volume**: The Geopolitical Landscape of the Central Powers  
**Entry**: The Confederated Baronies of Valoria  
**Author**: High Archivist Corvus, Arcanum Ministry of Truth  
**Subject**: The Mercenary State  

### I. Overview: The Business of War
Valoria is a paradox. It is a nation without a King, ruled by the "Iron Council" of five provincial Barons. To the outsider, it appears to be a military junta; in reality, it is a massive, decentralized corporation.
It is a "Second World" power—a land of jarring inequality. In the Guild districts, officers enjoy magical amenities and modern healthcare. A mile away, civilians live in tenements patched with corrugated iron, relying on water rations. The country does not export art or philosophy; it exports security.

**The Theology of Bellum**: The Valorians worship Bellum, not as a berserker, but as the Grand Strategist. He is the God of Logistics, Supply Lines, and Chess. His temples are quiet cartography halls where monks study maps rather than scripture. They believe that a battle is won before the first sword is drawn.

### II. The Five Provinces

#### 1. ARGENT (The Western Gateway)
- **Role**: Finance, Trade, Diplomacy.
- **The Vibe**: Rain-slicked cobblestones, the smell of expensive ink, guards in dress uniforms, wealth disparity.
- **Major City: Kovar**
    - The financial throat of the nation. Kovar is a city of grey limestone and iron railings, acting as the "foyer" where Valoria greets the wealthy merchants of Aurelia coming through Arcanum safe passage. The "Silent Quarter" is a banking district where noise is legally prohibited to aid the concentration of actuaries.
- **Town: Ledger**
    - A town entirely dedicated to bureaucracy. The air smells of cheap paper and coffee. Every third building is a notary office where mercenary contracts are audited and stamped.
- **Village: Dregs**
    - A sprawling slum in the drainage basin of the city. Here, the "porters"—laborers who carry heavy goods so merchants don't have to pay vehicle tariffs—live in shacks made of discarded crates.

#### 2. BULWARK (The Southern Shield)
- **Role**: Xylos Containment, Defense, Medicine.
- **The Vibe**: Concrete bunkers, constant sirens, grey skies, the metallic taste of blood and iodine.
- **Major City: Redoubt**
    - Redoubt is not built up, but down. It is a subterranean fortress-city designed to withstand magical fallout from the neighboring corrupted lands of Xylos. The population is 80% military; the streets are narrow tunnels lit by flickering gas lamps.
- **Town: Solace**
    - A "hospital town" ten miles behind the front lines. It is the only quiet place in the south. The economy revolves entirely around trauma surgery, prosthetics, and rehabilitation for broken soldiers.
- **Village: Verge**
    - The final habitation before the corruption starts. The villagers here are "Stalkers" who wear masked suits to scavenge rare, corrupted flora from the Xylos border to sell to black market alchemists.

#### 3. HEARTH (The Iron Coast)
- **Role**: Deep-Water Logistics, Heavy Import/Export, Ferrum Relations.
- **The Vibe**: Massive geometric architecture, cold sea spray mixed with coal smoke, the groaning of cranes, strict discipline.
- **Major City: CINDER (The Iron Harbor)**
    - **Location**: The Coast (Southern Peninsula).
    - **The Wonder**: The "Black Bay." The harbor water is permanently stained dark by oil and ash. It is the only port in Valoria deep enough to dock the massive "Leviathan-Class" ore haulers from Ferrum.
    - **The Vibe**: A city of cranes and blast furnaces built directly onto the docks. The factories here don't just make weapons; they eat the raw ore arriving by sea. The city never sleeps; the glow of the molten iron acts as a lighthouse for incoming ships.
    - **Strategic Importance**: This is the throat of the Valorian war machine. If Cinder closes, the tanks run out of steel in a week.
- **Town: DREDGE (The Slag Pit)**
    - **Location**: Inland (The Volcanic Ridge).
    - **The Role**: Extraction & Dumping.
    - **The Vibe**: A mining town built into the side of a dormant volcano (the "Earth-Wound"). This is where the raw minerals are dug out and where the toxic waste from Cinder is shipped back to be buried. It is a hot, miserable place where the sky is always grey.
    - **The Connection**: A direct, heavily armored rail line connects Dredge to Cinder, constantly ferrying coal down to the harbor.
- **Town: Respite (The Safe Passage)**
    - **Location**: Inland.
    - **The Role**: Land passage to Ferrum.
    - **The Vibe**: A trading defense border town. This is where trade to Ferrum on land happens. The danger here has been lowered through multiple expeditions and safe passage is created with help of both Valoria and Ferrum.
    - **The Connection**: A direct, heavily armored rail line connects Dredge and Cinder to Respite, and Respite has train to Ferrum.
- **Village: RUST**
    - **Location**: The Coastal Flats (The outskirts of Cinder).
    - **The Role**: The Scrapyard.
    - **The Vibe**: A sprawling shanty town where the "Ship-Breakers" live. When an ironclad is too old to sail, it is beached here. The villagers swarm it like ants, stripping it for copper and steel to sell back to the Cinder foundries.

#### 4. BRINE (The Eastern Coast)
- **Role**: Naval Operations, Shipping, Smuggling.
- **The Vibe**: Salt spray, rotting wood, dense fog, suspicious silences.
- **Major City: Lorn**
    - The naval hub. Lorn is a city of wooden boardwalks and damp fog. It serves as the transport hub for troops heading to foreign islands. It has a melancholic beauty, known for its "Widow’s Walk"—a promenade where spouses watch for ships that might never return.
- **Town: Hull**
    - A shipyard town. The sound of saws and hammers is constant, day and night. It is where the massive, flat-bottomed transport barges are constructed to ferry armies across the sea.
- **Village: Drift**
    - A shanty-town on stilts directly over the stagnant marsh water. It is a smuggler’s haven, where illicit goods (like drugs from Veridia) find their way into Valoria away from the prying eyes of the Guilds.

#### 5. AXIS (The Central Plateau)
- **Role**: Administration, Elite Training, Command.
- **The Vibe**: Cold, geometric, sterile, silent, imposing.
- **Major City: Vance**
    - The brain of the state. Vance is built on a high plateau, physically looking down on the other provinces. It is a city of brutalist concrete architecture and manicured parks. There are no slums here; you need a "Citizen's Permit" just to enter the city gates.
- **Town: Vellum**
    - A university town at the base of the plateau. It houses the War Colleges. It is filled with nervous students memorizing strategy books and tired professors. The vibe is one of intense academic pressure.
- **Village: Ascent**
    - A service village built on the steep incline of the road leading up to Vance. The houses are literally slanted. The people here are the maids, cooks, and drivers who commute up the hill to serve the elite, but are forbidden from living there.

### III. The Mercenary Charter (Global Relations)
The Ascenders Guild (HQ in Vance) strictly enforces the "Contract of Neutrality" to ensure Valoria remains profitable and not a global target.

- **The Neutrality Clause**: Valoria will never deploy troops in a war between two human nations (e.g., if Aurelia fights Bastion, Valoria stays neutral). This prevents the world from uniting against them.
- **The Crisis Mandate**:
    - **Xylos Containment**: The province of Bulwark receives billions in funding from foreign powers (Ferrum/Aurelia) to hold the line against monsters.
    - **Scar Breaches**: When magic tears reality open, the elite Ascenders of Axis are the only ones equipped to close them.
    - **Expeditionary Corps**: Scouts from Brine and Bulwark are often hired by central countries to explore the dangerous "Deep South" or Terra Incognita, treated as expendable assets by foreign kings.

## XXIII. PROTOCOLS OF KNOWLEDGE (Narrative Constraints)
*The following rules dictate the distribution of information within the setting. Characters should strictly adhere to these limitations based on their social standing.*

### 1. CIVILIAN KNOWLEDGE (The Common Awareness)
- **Scope**: 99% of the population (Farmers, Factory Workers, Merchants).
- **Magic Availability**: Civilians are fully aware of magic and rely on "Grey-Ware" (Magitech) for daily life (lights, trains, heating). They view it as a utility, like electricity, not a mystical wonder.
- **The Rank System**: They know the 10-point scale exists.
    - **Ranks 10-8**: Common. They see these people daily (guards, foremen).
    - **Ranks 7-6**: Mythic. Most civilians will live and die without ever seeing a Rank 6 Ascender. They are viewed as local celebrities or terrifying enforcers.
- **Cosmology**: They know three planes exist:
    - **Our Realm**: Where we live.
    - **The God Realm**: Where the Deities live (Civilians believe Gods cannot physically enter Orbis, which is why they send Avatars).
    - **The Abyss**: Where monsters come from.
- **Ignorance**: They do not know how one becomes an Ascender. To them, it is a mix of "being born special" and "government secrets."

### 2. ASCENDER KNOWLEDGE (The Guild Tier)
- **Scope**: Registered members of the Order of the Broken Chain.
- **The Boon Structure**: Only upon joining the Guild (or being trained by a Mentor) does one learn the mechanics of the Anchor, Reach, and Greed slots. This information is not in public libraries; it is trade secret material found only in Guild manuals.
- **The Spirit Echo (Sphere 3)**: All Ascenders are aware of the "Dream World" because they often feel its pressure when using magic.
- **The Second Law (Harmonic Resonance)**:
    - **Status**: Restricted.
    - **Unlock**: This knowledge is generally gatekept until an Ascender approaches Rank 7.
    - **Reason**: The Guild fears that if low-level mages understand how to perfectly "tune" their souls, they will advance too quickly, leading to dangerous mutations or unstable power spikes.

### 3. ELITE / FORBIDDEN KNOWLEDGE (The 0.1%)
- **Scope**: Rank 3+ Ascenders, High Priests, and Ancient Beings.
- **The First Law (Equivalent Distortion)**:
    - **The Secret**: The fact that Gods create the Abyss by filtering truth is the world's most dangerous secret. Only those close to Godhood know this. If the public knew their prayers were fueling the demons, society would collapse.
- **The Third Law (Conceptual Singularity)**:
    - **Unlock**: Rank 3.
    - **The Horror**: When an Ascender reaches Platinum Rank, they inherently feel the "Drain." They realize that to get stronger, they must kill others who hold the same concept.
- **Suppression**: The Guild and Governments actively hide the Second Law to prevent people from reaching the Third Law. They want to avoid a "War of All Against All" where mages hunt each other for power.
- **Deep Cosmology**:
    - **Sphere 4 (Earth) & Sphere 0 (The Architect)**: Unknown to almost everyone. Only the Gods, the oldest Progenitor Dragons, and perhaps the Guildmaster know of the "Stilled World" or the "Creator."
